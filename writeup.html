Unraveling Glucose Patterns: A 10-Day Look at Diabetes & Health

The Avengers: Neil Dandekar, Avi Mehta, Kevin Souder, Idhant Kumar

What Have You Done So Far?

  • We have implemented a bubble chart that shows each participant's average glucose level throughout the 10-day study, categorized by diabetes status, color-coded by gut health, and scaled for each participant's bubble by their BMI value, including a tooltip for each participant’s dot.
  • We also implemented a scrolly-telling into this visualization to show a time series throughout the 10 days to show the variability of the different diabetes statuses instead of a time slider and animation button.
  • We also added some annotations corresponding to some of the days of the study which appear as the user scrolls through the days, added more context to the webpage, and an additional context/transition paragraph to the next visualization.
  • We started to create a template for the second half/the rest of our project that’s still left to fill in completely. The template includes an introduction/landing page to first give a brief overview of the dataset/study before scrolling, which is the start of our next visualization. We plan to complete this as we progress through the project.

What Will Be the Most Challenging Part of Your Project to Design and Why?

  • One of our main challenges for our second visualization is how to effectively communicate/visualize how macros affect glucose levels and spikes in different diabetic categories in a way that’s interesting/fun for the user.
  • We can’t decide how to derive the best insights to display to the user as we’re scrolling through.
  • Based on our datasets and exploratory data analysis, we’re still trying to figure out how to group the participants in new ways and also describe things such as food macros without making the visuals cluttered or unintuitive.
  • In a more application sense, coding the scrollytelling and animations of our visualizations data and the incoming/disappearing text will be hard to incorporate into our HTML page. This will be hard since we haven’t had a lot of practice, besides Lab 8, with d3’s scrolly() and we’re trying to create an impressive final project.
